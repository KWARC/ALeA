That is correct! Okay, let's talk about the Pythagorean Theorem.

The Pythagorean Theorem states that in a right-angled triangle, the square of the hypothenuse (the longest side, opposite the right angle) is equal to the sum of the squares of the two other sides. Often, this is expressed as a² + b² = c².
